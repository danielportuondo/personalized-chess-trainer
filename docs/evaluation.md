# Threshold evaluation: validating the pipeline's tuning against real data

The trainer's extraction and curation thresholds were hand-picked. This memo tests
them against the full local dataset (4.5 years of my own chess.com games analyzed
by the Python CLI at depth 12) and recommends keep/adjust per threshold.

Every number below is generated by [`analysis/evaluate_thresholds.py`](../analysis/evaluate_thresholds.py):

```
uv run python analysis/evaluate_thresholds.py     # ~4.5 min, mostly the Stockfish pass
```

**Verdicts**

| Threshold | Where | Verdict | Key evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| `CPL_THRESHOLD = 150` | `extract.py` / `extract.ts` | **Keep** | Sits at p87 of the move-loss distribution; lowering to 100 mostly adds low-teachability "other" mistakes |
| `UNIQUE_GAP_CP = 100` | `pipeline.ts` | **Lower to 50** (and gate after curation) | Flags 63.7% of all puzzles and 30.5% of drillable ones; 50cp keeps genuinely dual-solution filtering while saving ~44% of rejected drills |
| Curation gates + `difficultyScore` | `curate.ts` | **Keep** | Gates concentrate exactly on tactics (86% of missed mates pass, 4% of "other"); ordering is monotone easy-to-hard |

## 1. Dataset

| | |
|---|---|
| Raw games fetched (chess.com, user `dportuondo`) | 6,859 (blitz 5,612 / rapid 1,219 / bullet 28) |
| Games analyzed (Stockfish depth 12) | 6,817 |
| Date range of analyzed games | 2022-02-21 to 2026-07-13 |
| Player move evaluations | 179,021 |
| Stored puzzles (cpl ≥ 150, eval-capped, deduped) | 20,605 |

## 2. Methodology

- **Reproduction first.** The script re-runs the extraction query (threshold + eval-cap
  + position-dedupe) from scratch and reproduces the stored puzzles table **exactly**:
  20,605 puzzles and identical motif counts (797 / 926 / 5,412 / 4,272 / 9,198). Motif
  classification is imported from the reference implementation (`src/chess_trainer/profile.py`),
  not reimplemented.
- **Curation logic is a Python port** of `web/src/curate.ts` (which has no Python
  counterpart). The port is verified at startup against all 12 fixture cases of
  `web/tests/curate.test.ts` (the script aborts on any mismatch), and three real
  puzzles were additionally checked by hand on a board (a Qf2+/Ra1# mate-in-2, a
  Rb1+/Rxc1# back-ranker, and a Qh8+ then Bxc5 rook-for-bishop win, +2 net after
  the scripted recapture).
- **Ambiguity gate**: Stockfish 18, MultiPV=2, depth 12, single thread (matching the
  CLI's engine options), on a seeded random sample of **3,000 of the 20,605 puzzles**
  (`seed=42`). Engine pass: **248.9s total, 0.08s/position**.

## 3. `CPL_THRESHOLD = 150` — keep

Distribution of centipawn loss over all 179,021 evaluated moves:

```
p50=18   p75=68   p90=188   p95=353   p99=9279  (mate-bounded, see limitations)

       [0, 25) 100529 ( 56.2%) ########################################
      [25, 50)  22661 ( 12.7%) #########
     [50, 100)  22683 ( 12.7%) #########
    [100, 150)  10491 (  5.9%) ####
    [150, 200)   5813 (  3.2%) ##
    [200, 300)   6012 (  3.4%) ##
    [300, 500)   5118 (  2.9%) ##
   [500, 1000)   2584 (  1.4%) #
       >= 1000   3130 (  1.7%) #
```

150cp is the **87.3rd percentile** of move losses — comfortably above the bulk of
ordinary moves (p75 = 68cp), where genuine inaccuracies blur into depth-12 eval
noise. Sensitivity of yield and motif mix (after the eval-cap filter and position
dedupe):

| T | puzzles | per game | median cpl | mate-missed | mate-allowed | hanging | material | other |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 100 | 30,137 | 4.42 | 201 | 2.6% | 3.1% | 22.1% | 18.8% | **53.4%** |
| **150** | **20,605** | **3.02** | **279** | 3.9% | 4.5% | 26.3% | 20.7% | **44.6%** |
| 200 | 15,186 | 2.23 | 354 | 5.2% | 6.1% | 29.9% | 21.5% | 37.3% |
| 300 | 9,440 | 1.38 | 485 | 8.4% | 9.8% | 33.8% | 20.8% | 27.2% |

- Dropping to 100 adds ~9,500 puzzles, but the marginal mass is dominated by
  "other" (positional slips with no concrete refutation) — the mix degrades from
  44.6% to 53.4% other.
- Raising to 200/300 purifies the mix, but supply is not a constraint (see §5:
  5,370 drillable puzzles survive curation at T=150) and the profile benefits from
  coverage; the curation layer already removes the non-tactical mass downstream
  (only 4% of "other" puzzles are ever served).

**Recommendation: keep 150.** It cleanly separates "real mistake" from eval noise,
and the tactical-purity concern is better solved by curation than by the threshold.

## 4. `UNIQUE_GAP_CP = 100` — lower to 50, and check uniqueness after curation

MultiPV=2 depth-12 gap (best minus second-best move, cp, side-to-move POV) over the
3,000-puzzle sample (1 position had a single legal reply and can never be flagged):

```
p10=5   p25=19   p50=59   p75=158   p90=320

       [0, 25)    901 ( 30.0%) ########################################
      [25, 50)    459 ( 15.3%) ####################
      [50, 75)    320 ( 10.7%) ##############
     [75, 100)    230 (  7.7%) ##########
    [100, 150)    300 ( 10.0%) #############
    [150, 200)    228 (  7.6%) ##########
    [200, 300)    227 (  7.6%) ##########
    [300, 500)    166 (  5.5%) #######
       >= 500    168 (  5.6%) #######
```

The median gap (59cp) is *below* the current 100cp bar — the gate rejects the
majority case. Flag rates by candidate threshold, split by whether the curation
layer would serve the puzzle at all:

| group | n | gap<50 | gap<75 | **gap<100 (current)** | gap<150 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| all puzzles | 3,000 | 45.3% | 56.0% | **63.7%** (95% CI 61.9–65.4) | 73.7% |
| drillable | 789 | 17.2% | 22.9% | **30.5%** | 40.6% |
| — goal=mate | 74 | 14.9% | 16.2% | **17.6%** | 17.6% |
| — goal=material | 715 | 17.5% | 23.6% | **31.9%** | 42.9% |
| not drillable | 2,211 | 55.4% | 67.8% | **75.5%** | 85.5% |

This confirms the live observation (34/67 ≈ 51% flagged, whose 95% CI ≈ 39–62%
overlaps our estimate) and sharpens it:

1. **The gate is too aggressive where it matters.** Of puzzles that pass curation —
   the only ones a user would ever see — 30.5% are discarded at 100cp. A solution
   that is 50–100cp better than the runner-up is a clearly best move for drilling
   purposes; rejecting those costs ~1 in 8 drillable puzzles (13.3% of drillable
   inventory sits in the 50–100cp band) with no fairness benefit.
2. **Most flags land on puzzles curation discards anyway.** 75.5% of non-drillable
   puzzles get flagged, but they were never going to be served — meaning ~74% of
   the per-puzzle MultiPV engine calls are spent on positions the drill screen
   already excludes. Running `curateLine` (pure, no engine) *first* and
   uniqueness-checking only survivors cuts analysis-time engine work ~4x.
3. **Mate puzzles need a different rule, not a gap.** Mate scores are bounded
   (mate-in-2 = 9998 vs mate-in-3 = 9997), so a position with two mating moves has
   gap ≈ 1cp and is always flagged — yet either move "solves" the puzzle. That is
   why the mate flag-rate barely moves across thresholds (14.9% → 17.6%). An
   "accept any mating move" rule would rescue these outright.

**Recommendation: lower to 50** (drillable rejection drops 30.5% → 17.2%, keeping
the truly dual-solution cases out), reorder the pipeline to curate-then-verify, and
special-case dual mates. n=789 drillable in the sample; the 50cp figure's rough 95%
CI is ±2.6pp.

## 5. Curation gates + `difficultyScore` — keep

Over all 20,605 stored puzzles, the port of `curate.ts` (MOVE_CAP=3, MATE_MOVE_CAP=4,
MATERIAL_GAIN_MIN=2) marks **5,370 drillable (26.1%)** — mate 524 (9.8%) / material
4,846 (90.2%); user-move depth 1: 36.6%, 2: 39.8%, 3: 22.8%, 4: 0.8%. The gates
select exactly what they claim to:

| motif | drillable | rate |
|---|---:|---:|
| missed forced mate | 684 / 797 | 85.8% |
| missed win of material | 2,990 / 4,272 | 70.0% |
| hanging piece | 1,216 / 5,412 | 22.5% |
| allowed forced mate | 108 / 926 | 11.7% |
| other | 372 / 9,198 | **4.0%** |

Tactical motifs pass at high rates; positional "other" mistakes — which have no
verifiable payoff to drill — are almost entirely excluded. Defensive motifs
(allowed mate, hanging your own piece) pass rarely, as expected: their best line is
usually a quiet save with no material payoff. The 4-move allowance is nearly
inert (0.8% of drills), so the caps are not binding.

`difficultyScore = userMoves*10 − forcingRatio*5 − min(cpl,500)/100` over drillable puzzles:

```
min=0.00  p25=2.13  p50=11.52  p75=15.81  max=33.75

        [0, 5)   1953 ( 36.4%) ########################################
       [5, 10)     10 (  0.2%) #
      [10, 15)   1815 ( 33.8%) #####################################
      [15, 20)    324 (  6.0%) #######
      [20, 25)    977 ( 18.2%) ####################
      [25, 30)    246 (  4.6%) #####
      [30, 35)     45 (  0.8%) #
```

The distribution is bands at multiples of 10 — the score is, by construction,
line-depth ordering with forcing-ness and blunder-size as tiebreaks (Spearman rho
vs. user moves +0.938, vs. forcing ratio −0.634, vs. cpl −0.373). The near-empty
[5,10) band is itself a sanity signal: one-move drills almost always end in a
capture or mate (forcingRatio=1), pinning their scores below 5. Ordering by score
quartile behaves as intended — sessions warm up on one-move refutations of big
blunders and end on three-move lines:

| quartile (served order) | n | mean user moves | mean cpl |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Q1 (score ≤ 2.13) | 1,346 | 1.00 | 2,628 |
| Q2 (score ≤ 11.52) | 1,343 | 1.54 | 2,043 |
| Q3 (score ≤ 15.81) | 1,339 | 2.00 | 779 |
| Q4 (score ≤ 33.75) | 1,342 | 2.98 | 1,717 |

(The Q4 cpl uptick is deep *mate* lines: mate-bounded cpl ≈ 9,700+ inflates the raw
mean, while the score itself clamps cpl at 500 — an ordering feature, not a bug.)

**Recommendation: keep.** The gates and ordering do what they were designed to do.
The one real validation gap: difficulty is defined structurally, not empirically —
correlating the score against actual solve/fail outcomes from drill sessions is the
natural next iteration once enough review history accumulates.

## 6. Limitations

- **One player's games.** All thresholds are calibrated on a single account
  (~82% blitz, 4.5 years of rating drift). The numbers describe this corpus, not
  chess players generally — appropriate for a personalized trainer, but the exact
  percentages will differ per user.
- **Depth-12 noise.** Both cpl and the MultiPV gap inherit depth-12 jitter; gaps
  near a threshold are unstable under re-analysis. The web app also runs a WASM
  Stockfish build while this analysis used native Stockfish 18, so live flag rates
  will differ modestly from the sample estimates (they do: 51% live vs 63.7% here,
  overlapping CIs, same conclusion).
- **Mate-bounded cpl.** Mate evals are clamped to ±10,000 before cpl arithmetic, so
  cpl values ≳9,000 mean "a forced mate was on the board", not literal centipawns
  (visible as p99=9,279 and the Q4 cpl mean). Fine for thresholding; misleading if
  averaged naively — which is why the profile screens cap each mistake's
  contribution to displayed averages at 1,000cp.
- **Sample sizes.** The MultiPV pass sampled 3,000/20,605 puzzles (all-puzzle CI
  ±1.8pp); the drillable-mate cell is n=74, directional only. Sections 1–3 use the
  full dataset and are exactly reproducible; the engine section is seeded but
  engine-version- and machine-dependent.
- **No outcome data.** Drillability and difficulty are validated structurally and
  against the engine, not against human solve rates — the DB has no per-drill
  success history yet.
